Study

Do you work or are you a student?

I’m currently a second-year master’s student, actually. I’m studying computer science, and most of my time is spent either doing research or working on my thesis. It’s pretty demanding, but I enjoy it because I get to explore topics that I’m really passionate about. Right now, I’m focusing on AI, which is both challenging and exciting.

What subject are you studying?

I’m studying computer science, and my major field is actually focused on the ethical issues surrounding generative AI. It’s a really fascinating area because the technology is evolving so quickly, but at the same time, there are a lot of concerns about things like bias, misinformation, and copyright. My research looks at how we can make these systems more fair and responsible, which I think is becoming more and more important as AI becomes part of our life.

Why did you choose that subject?

Well, to be honest, I got into computer science because I was fascinated by the idea of creating something like Iron Man’s AI—Jarvis. At the beginning, it was all about the cool factor, you know, building smart systems that can talk and think like humans. But as I dug deeper into the field, I started to fall in love with designing AI algorithms and understanding how they actually work.

Now I find it incredibly rewarding to not only develop intelligent systems but also think about their social impact.

What would you like to do in the future?

Honestly, I’m still figuring it out, but I have a couple of ideas in mind. One option is to pursue a PhD and eventually become a university lecturer, because I really enjoy research and sharing knowledge. On the other hand, I’m also considering working as an AI engineer in a tech company, since that would give me a chance to apply what I’ve learned to real-world problems. Either way, I’d like to stay in the field of AI, because it’s such an exciting and fast-moving area.

What are the most popular subjects in China?

Well, from what I’ve seen, computer science is definitely one of the most popular subjects in China right now. In particular, areas like generative AI and large language models—like GPT-4—have attracted a huge amount of attention. A lot of students are really drawn to these cutting-edge technologies because they see a lot of potential for innovation and career opportunities. It’s becoming more and more common for students to choose majors related to AI, data science, or machine learning.

Do you think it’s important to choose a subject you like?

Absolutely, I think it’s really important to choose a subject you genuinely enjoy. When you're interested in something, learning doesn't feel like playing games—it becomes exciting. As the saying goes, interest is the best teacher, and I truly believe that. If you’re passionate about what you’re studying, you’re more likely to stay motivated, dig deeper, and do well in the long run. So for me, choosing a subject you like makes all the difference.

Are you looking forward to working?

To be honest, I do look forward to working, and I have a lot of expectations for it. I think it’ll be exciting to solve real problems and see the impact of what I do. But at the same time, I feel like I’m not fully prepared yet. There’s a big difference between school and the workplace, and I know it’ll take time to adjust. So while I’m excited about the future.

Do you like your subject?

Yes, absolutely! I really enjoy studying computer science. There’s always something new to learn, especially in the field of AI, which is evolving so fast. I find it exciting to explore how machines can learn and create. It’s a subject that keeps me curious and motivated, so I’d definitely say I love it.

Do you prefer to study in the mornings or in the afternoons?

I definitely prefer studying in the morning. If I’ve had a good night’s sleep, I usually feel fresh and focused when I wake up. That’s when I’m most productive, especially for things that require deep thinking, like reading papers or writing code. In the afternoon, I tend to feel a bit sleepy, so it’s harder for me to concentrate. That’s why I try to get the most important work done earlier in the day.
